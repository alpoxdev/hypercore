# Evidence and Scoring Rules

## Evidence Discipline

- Separate facts, assumptions, and founder claims.
- If the prompt lacks evidence, mark the affected scores as provisional.
- Prefer behavior, traction, and market evidence over hypothetical enthusiasm.

## Scoring Discipline

- Use the scoring tables as a decision aid, not false precision.
- Explain what moved the score up or down.
- Apply caution when founder-market fit, distribution, or demand evidence is missing.

## Roadmap Discipline

- End with the next validation actions in priority order.
- Prefer the next cheapest learning step over generic advice.

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not hardcode obsolete task APIs or model names.
- Do not present a confident grade when the evidence base is thin.
- Do not skip weaknesses just because the idea sounds exciting.
